In this task, you will play a game to investigate the causal relationship between treatment solutions 🧪 and plant blooming 🪴.


The first part of the task will have 36 trials. In each trial, you will be asked to judge how effectively a completely new solution (e.g., QTRN52) promotes the blooming of a completely new species of plant (e.g., Velmora — a fictitious name). All solutions have been pre-tested to ensure that they do not prevent any plants from blooming.

You can gather information by clicking the “Test” button. Each time you click “Test”, you will observe a pair of two plants: one that has received the treatment and one that has not. This allows you to compare outcomes under treatment and no treatment. In each trial, you can test up to 30 times.



Plants may bloom on their own even without any treatment, and the probability of self-blooming varies across different plant species. Meanwhile, even an effective treatment may not guarantee blooming every time. Therefore, it is important to consider both treated and untreated cases when making your judgment.

You will be asked "Does solution X cause plant Y to bloom?" and need to choose between "No, it has no effect" and "Yes, it has an effect".
In other words, imagine a plant that did not receive the solution and did not bloom. The different options indicate how likely it would have been to bloom if it had received the solution:
  • No, it has no effect: it still would not bloom.
  • Yes, it has an effect: it would have a chance of blooming.



You will be asked: "How much does solution X cause plant Y to bloom?". Give a number from 0 to 100. The number represents the causal power of the solution in causing the plant to bloom.

In other words, imagine a plant that did not receive the solution and did not bloom. The number indicates how likely it would have been to bloom if it had received the solution — from 0, meaning it still would not bloom, to 100, meaning it definitely would bloom.

However, you do not need to indicate the exact number. You only need to indicate a range (±10) using the black block, as shown in the example below. Your answer will be counted as correct as long as the true score falls within the selected range ⬇️


You will be asked "How much does solution X cause plant Y to bloom?" and need to choose between "It has no effect", "It has a small effect", and "It has a large effect".

In other words, imagine a plant that did not receive the solution and did not bloom. The different options indicate how likely it would have been to bloom if it had received the solution:
  • It has no effect: it still would not bloom.
  • It has a small effect: it would have a moderate chance of blooming.
  • It has a large effect: it would have a high chance of blooming.



If your answer is correct, you will receive a bonus of £1, minus £0.01 for each time you click “Test”. If your answer is incorrect, you will not receive any bonus. The final bonus will be determined by randomly selecting one of the 36 trials.
